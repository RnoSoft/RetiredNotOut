The Albert Dock, Liverpool.

This photo was taken on a cloudy day during a visit to Liverpool in early October, while walking along the Liverpool waterfront around the Pier Head. Several other of my daily photos were taken on that walk.

Further information on the Albert Dock can be found here and here.

The Albert Dock is a complex of dock buildings in Liverpool. It was opened in 1846 and finally closed in 1972. It then remained derelict for almost ten years. During that time there was a big debate about what to do with it. A lot of people in power wanted to demolish it, some even suggested building a tower block there. Luckily sense rained, the buildings where renovated and it became a public attraction. Well worth a visit.

If you are thirsty after you visit, I suggest the Baltic Fleet pub, just down the road. It’s in Camra’s Good Beer guide and the real ale is excellent.
