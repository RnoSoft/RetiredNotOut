![Seacombe ferry landing stage.]

Today I decided to walk along the Mersey promenade from the Seacombe Ferry landing stage to New Brighton. It's a lovely walk with no traffic allowed on the prom. There are fine views across the Mersey to the North Liverpool docks. In the distance you can see Liverpool's Pier Head.

After the walk, I met my friend John in the Bow-Legged Beagle for some real ale.

The weather was not ideal for photography, but this is my journal, so I've decided to publish some of them, as a memory of the walk. Maybe next time I do it, it'll be in sunny weather so I can get some better photos. 

The Isle of Man ferry was in port.

![Isle of Man Ferry berthed in Liverpool.]

Liverpool Pier Head.

![Liverpool Pier Head from Seacombe, one.]

The [*Atlantis Dweller*](https://www.marinetraffic.com/en/ais/details/ships/shipid:379171/mmsi:311000810/imo:9429742/vessel:ATLANTIS_DWELLER){:target="_blank"} was berthed at the Pier Head. If you follow the link, you'll find a page on the Marine Traffic app (John told me about it the other day) describing the ship. It contains details of the vessel and its current location. As I write this, it's berthed in Aberdeen.

![Liverpool Pier Head from Seacombe, two.]

While I was walking along the prom the *Atlantis Dweller* left the Pier Head, heading out to open sea.

![The Atlantis Dweller leaving the Pier Head.]

The ship approaching the Isle of Man ferry.

![The Atlantis Dweller passing the Isle of Man Ferry.]

The ship approaching Everton's new football stadium.

![The Atlantis Dweller approaching Everton's new football ground.]

I'm no expert at wildlife photography, but I did manage to capture this seagull standing on the prom's iron railing, watching the world go by.

![Bird of the rail.]

A bit further on, the Mersey Ferry, the [Royal Iris](https://www.marinetraffic.com/en/ais/details/ships/shipid:190000/mmsi:235024007/imo:0/vessel:ROYAL_IRIS){:target="_blank"} passed by. In the following photo it's passing Everton's new football stadium.

![Royal Iris ferry passing Everton's new stadium.]

Next, it's between the Victoria Tower and the Tobacco Warehouse at Stanley Dock.

![Roral Iris ferry between the Victoria Tower and the Tobacco Warehouse.]

Soon after it's approaching the Isle of Man ferry.

![Royal Iris ferry approaching the Isle of Man Ferry.]

The first pub along the prom is [The Ferry](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/ferry-egremont-131942){:target="_blank"} at Egremont. A pub I've never visited and probably never will (better pubs are available).

![The Ferry pub at Egremont]

While walking along the prom, I was passed by many cyclists. Just past The Ferry pub several in brightly coloured jackets headed my way. 

There were also lots of runners, and people just taking a stroll. Today was a Thursday so the prom was quiet. It can get very busy at weekends.

![Cyclists passing the Ferryr Inn at Seacombe.]

Looking back towards the Ferry pub and the Pier Head.

![Looking back towards the Ferry Inn.]

Various views back towards Liverpool and the Pier Head.

![Liverpool waterfront, one.]

![LIverpool water front, two.]

![Liverpool water front, three.]

![Liverpool water front, four.]

As you get closer to New Brighton, keep looking up the side streets for [The Magazine](https://camra.org.uk/pubs/magazine-hotel-new-brighton-132001){:target="_blank"} pub, it's easy to spot. It's often in the beer guide (it's in the 2026 edition) and has won numerous Camra Wirral awards. I had a pint of Trappers Hat bitter (brewed by Brimstage) and a good rest.

While walking along the prom I was hoping to see the Isle of Man ferry depart. It left while I was supping my pint. When I got back to the prom you could see it disappearing into the distance, too far away for a photo. Not only that, up-river I could see a roll on/roll off ferry berthing at the Birkenhead ferry terminal. I missed two ships passing.

![Magazine Hotel, New Brighton]

There are benches all along the prom, for you to have a rest. Many near New Brighton have memorials on them.

![Benches with memorials, one]

![Memorial bench.]

The Seaforth Dock is almost opposite New Brighton.

![Seacombe dock.]

As I get older (I'm seventy) walking is becoming more and more of a problem, I wrote about this [here](/journal/centre-of-slaithwaite-june-2025). The walk along the prom is around three miles on a hard surface. It took me days to get over it.

When I finally got to the Bow-Legged Beagle it was just opening, I was one of its first customers that day.
